# Pillar × census tract — logic + plain English (Gemini framing)

Personal notes — not part of the shipped product narrative.

**Distance column:** Values are the **default capture distances** wired in code (`data_sources/radius_profiles.py`, pillar modules, `crime_api`, `gee_api`, etc.). **`area_type`** (e.g. `urban_core` vs `suburban`) and **`location_scope`** (`neighborhood` vs `city`) change several pillars—this table uses **`urban_core` + `neighborhood` scope** where `get_radius_profile` applies, and notes **variable-by-class** rows where the code branches.

| Pillar | Uses tract polygon as scoring window? | Tract-related logic (what the code actually does) | Plain English context / why it matters | Signal capture distance(s) |
|--------|----------------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------|----------------------------------------|------------------------------|
| **diversity** | Yes — primary | All mix/entropy inputs are ACS variables for the containing tract of the pin. | Who lives in your immediate neighborhood. Demographics don't change wildly door-to-door, so it uses the official, local Census boundary to gauge the neighborhood's socioeconomic and cultural mix. | **No search radius** — signals are for the **containing census tract polygon** only (pin → tract GEOID). |
| **housing_value** | Yes — primary | Affordability and housing stock signals come from `get_housing_data` on that same containing tract. | The local real estate market. It looks at housing stock, price trends, and affordability within that specific geographic pocket using official local housing records. | **No search radius** — same **tract polygon** as diversity. |
| **economic_security** | No | Tract is only used to look up CBSA (and county) codes. Labor tables are fetched for the CBSA (metro). | The regional job market. People rarely work on the exact block where they sleep. It zooms out to the entire city/metro labor market because that's the economic reality of where the income is actually generated. | **No distance ring** — **metro CBSA** (and county fallback), keyed from the tract record, not a buffer around the pin. |
| **social_fabric** | Partial | Stability / mobility uses tract-level Census. Civic / participation uses a geodesic disk (radius) from the pin. | Split personality. It checks if your immediate neighbors move away constantly (using local block data), but scores community perks like libraries or public spaces based on a realistic walking distance around your front door. | **Tract:** stability / mobility (no disk). **Civic OSM disk:** density-driven **600 m** (≥10k people/sq mi), **1200 m** (≥2.5k), else **3000 m**, then **capped at 1200 m** when `area_type` is `urban_core`, `urban_residential`, or `suburban` (`pillars/social_fabric.py`). |
| **community_safety** | Partial (denominator only) | Crime is mapped to a disk around the pin; population is estimated by intersecting overlapping tract populations with that disk. | Avoids the "bad block" trap. Instead of judging safety by an arbitrary government line, it draws a circle around the pin to catch nearby incidents, then uses the surrounding population to calculate an accurate crime rate. | **Crime + population disk (varies by `area_type`):** `urban_core` **800 m**; `urban_residential` **1000 m**; `suburban` **2000 m**; `exurban` **5000 m**; `rural` **8000 m**; else **1500 m** (`data_sources/crime_api.py` `community_safety_crime_radius_m`). Denominator may **areal-weight many tracts** intersecting that same disk (`census_api.estimate_community_safety_disk_population`). |
| **neighborhood_amenities** | No | No tract geometry. OSM pulls use meter radii from the pin only. | Pure walkability. It completely ignores imaginary lines and just counts the coffee shops, bars, restaurants, and retail options you can actually walk to from your front door. | **`neighborhood` scope:** **1200 m** query disk + **800 m** “walkable” band for scoring (`get_radius_profile`). **`city` scope:** **1500 m** + **1000 m**. |
| **active_outdoors** | No | No tract geometry. Local / trail / regional windows are disks from the pin. | Access to parks and recreation. It draws circles from the pin to see if you can walk to a local playground, bike to a massive park, or easily drive to a regional hiking trail. | **`urban_core`:** **800 m** local parks/green, **15 km** trails, **15 km** regional water/camping (`get_radius_profile`). **Suburban:** **1500 m** / **15 km** / **15 km**. **Rural/exurban/unknown:** **2000 m** / **15 km** / **50 km**. Main may also precompute **5 km** tree canopy for shared cache used with natural/outdoors. |
| **public_transit_access** | No | No tract geometry. Transit search uses routes radius from the pin. | Commute options. It measures the actual physical distance to the nearest subway lines, train stations, or bus routes from the address. A subway line 50 meters away counts, even if it's technically across a neighborhood border. | **`urban_core`:** **3000 m** routes search. **Suburban:** **2000 m**. **Exurban/rural/unknown:** **1500 m**. |
| **healthcare_access** | No | No tract geometry. OSM + fallbacks use facility / pharmacy radii from the pin. | Running health errands. Measures the physical distance to the nearest ER, urgent care, or pharmacy, because a hospital doesn't care what neighborhood boundary it sits across. | **`urban_core`:** **5000 m** facilities + **2000 m** pharmacies (`get_radius_profile`). **Suburban / exurban / rural** use larger pairs (10k/3k, 15k/5k, 20k/8k). **Fallback hospital DB** can add candidates out to **60 km** if OSM returns nothing (`pillars/healthcare_access.py`). |
| **built_environment** | No | No tract geometry for visual sampling; Census year-built is a point → tract attribute lookup. | Vibe check. It scans the actual streets immediately around you for architectural diversity, but pulls the official local records just to see when the buildings on your block were built. | **Architecture / charm / NRHP window:** **`architectural_diversity_radius_m`** — **2000 m** default from profile (same as `natural_beauty` profile’s arch key). **Beauty enhancers (OSM):** **1500 m**. With **`neighborhood` scope**, profile also sets **1000 m** tree canopy radius (used more by natural pillar, but returned on the shared profile dict). |
| **natural_beauty** | No | No tract geometry; radii from profile + Google Earth Engine satellite windows from the pin. | The view from above. Uses satellite data to measure how much green canopy, tree cover, or open water is within a short radius of your specific coordinate. | **Primary canopy / GEE:** profile **`tree_canopy_radius_m`** — **1000 m** with `neighborhood` scope + `urban_core` (else **800 m** for `urban_residential` / historic types, up to **2000 m** in sprawl types). **Multi-radius canopy blend:** **400 m**, **1000 m**, **2000 m** (`MULTI_RADIUS_CANOPY`). **Canopy ladder** in code can widen to **2 km** or **3 km** for sparse canopy (urban_core, non-neighborhood scope paths). **Parks (OSM):** often **400 m**, fallback **1000 m**, suburban/exurban extension **2000 m**. **Street trees:** **1000 m**. **Green view (GVI):** up to **~1200 m** cap. **Landcover micro context:** **250 m**. Shared prep **5 km** canopy when that path is active. |
| **air_travel_access** | No | No tract geometry. Airport discovery uses a large km radius from the pin. | The airport run. It just calculates how long it takes to drive or transit to the major regional airports (JFK, LGA, EWR). Administrative neighborhood lines are completely irrelevant here. | **100 km** airport search radius (`get_radius_profile` — all area types). |
| **quality_education** | No | No tract geometry. School discovery uses a mile-radius disk from the pin. | Local school options. It looks at all educational options within a reasonable driving or walking radius of the house, which mirrors how parents actually evaluate school access. | **`urban_core`:** **1.5 mi** (~2.4 km). **Suburban:** **2.0 mi**. **Exurban/rural/unknown:** **3.0 mi** (`get_radius_profile`). |
| **climate_risk** | No | No tract polygon; hazards are evaluated from lat/lon against continuous hazard layers. | Environmental reality. Mother nature does not care about zoning lines. It evaluates if your specific plot of land sits in a flood zone, a wildfire risk area, or a heat island based on raw physical geography. | **Heat (LST UHI):** **500 m** local buffer vs **5000 m** regional buffer (`get_heat_exposure_lst` defaults). **Air (S5P proxy):** **2000 m** buffer (`get_air_quality_aer_ai`). **Flood:** **point-in-polygon** NFHL lookup (no fixed search radius). **30-yr warming trend:** **5000 m** TerraClimate buffer (`get_climate_trend_terraclimate`). |
